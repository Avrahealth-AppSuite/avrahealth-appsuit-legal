1. Introduction

AvraHealth ("we," "our," or "us") built VascularCoder as a medical billing code reference tool for vascular proceduralists and billing professionals. This Privacy Policy explains how we handle information when you use our iOS application ("the App").

Our core principle: Your data stays on your device. VascularCoder does not transmit data to our servers because we do not operate any servers. We do not collect, store, or have access to any of your data.

2. Information You Enter

VascularCoder allows you to enter and manage the following types of information locally on your device:

  • Encounter data: Optional patient name, optional Medical Record Number (MRN), service dates, surgeon role, and encounter labels
  • Procedure codes: CPT codes, modifiers, laterality, and associated RVU values
  • Diagnosis codes: ICD-10 codes linked to procedures
  • Billing sessions: Grouped encounters with dates and RVU totals
  • Preferences: Default surgeon role, Medicare locality, display settings, favorite codes, recent codes, tab order, and modifier preferences

All of this information is stored locally on your device using on-device storage (UserDefaults). We do not have access to any of this data.

3. iCloud Sync

If you have iCloud enabled on your device, VascularCoder may sync certain data across your Apple devices using Apple's iCloud (NSUbiquitousKeyValueStore), including:

  • Favorite and recent codes
  • Billing session history
  • User preferences and settings
  • Tab order customization

This sync is managed entirely by Apple's iCloud infrastructure. We do not operate the servers involved, and we do not have access to your iCloud data. Apple's iCloud data handling is governed by Apple's Privacy Policy.

You can disable iCloud sync for VascularCoder in your device's Settings > Apple Account > iCloud.

4. Data We Do NOT Collect

VascularCoder does not collect, transmit, or share:

  • Personal identification information (name, email, phone number)
  • Location data
  • Device identifiers or advertising identifiers
  • Analytics or usage data
  • Crash reports or diagnostics
  • Health data via HealthKit
  • Contacts, photos, or calendar data
  • Browsing or search history
  • Any data to third-party analytics, advertising, or tracking services

We do not use any third-party SDKs for analytics, advertising, crash reporting, or tracking.

5. Third-Party Services

VascularCoder does not integrate with any third-party services, APIs, or servers. The only external service interaction is Apple's iCloud sync (if enabled by you), which is governed by Apple's terms and privacy policy.

6. Data Export

VascularCoder allows you to export billing session data as CSV or PDF files. These exports are generated locally on your device and shared using the iOS share sheet. We do not receive copies of exported data. You are responsible for the security of any exported files.

7. Data Security

All data is stored locally on your device using iOS platform storage mechanisms and is protected by your device's passcode, Face ID, or Touch ID. iCloud-synced data is encrypted in transit and at rest by Apple's iCloud security infrastructure.

Since we do not operate servers or collect your data, there is no risk of a data breach on our end.

8. Data Retention and Deletion

Your data is stored on your device for as long as you choose to keep it. You can delete data at any time by:

  • Deleting individual billing sessions from the History view
  • Clearing favorite or recent codes
  • Deleting the app entirely, which removes all locally stored data

To remove iCloud-synced data, disable iCloud sync for VascularCoder or manage your iCloud storage through your device's Settings.

9. Children's Privacy

VascularCoder is a professional medical billing reference tool and is not directed at children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect information from children. The App is intended for use by medical professionals and billing staff.

10. HIPAA Considerations

VascularCoder is a local-only tool that does not transmit Protected Health Information (PHI) to us or any third party. However, if you choose to enter patient-identifiable information (such as patient names or MRNs), you are responsible for ensuring your use of the App complies with HIPAA and any other applicable regulations. We recommend:

  • Using device-level security (passcode, biometrics) to protect your device
  • Being mindful of optional patient identifiers you enter
  • Managing exported files in accordance with your organization's policies
